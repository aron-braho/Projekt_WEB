The Little Things

Kern County Deputy Sheriff Joe "Deke" Deacon is sent to Los Angeles for what should have been a quick evidence-gathering assignment. Instead, he becomes embroiled in the search for a serial killer who is terrorizing the city. Leading the hunt, L.A. Sheriff Department Sergeant Jim Baxter, impressed with Deke's instincts, enlists his help unofficially. But as they track the killer, Baxter is unaware that the investigation is dredging up echoes of Deke's past, uncovering unsettling secrets that could threaten more than just the case.

Parents' Guide

Violence: Crime scenes showing victims of a serial killer; some blood and disturbing imagery; a sequence involving a tense confrontation in the desert.

Language: Frequent strong profanity and police jargon.

Sex: Brief references to sexual assault (thematic to the crimes); no graphic sexual scenes.

Substances: Scenes showing characters drinking alcohol and smoking cigarettes.

Intensity: A dark, brooding atmosphere with heavy psychological weight; themes of obsession and haunting guilt.

Themes: The price of justice, the weight of the past, and the moral "little things" that can destroy a person.

Messages: Focuses on how obsession can blur the lines between right and wrong.

Verdict: Rated R for violent images and language. Recommended for mature audiences.
