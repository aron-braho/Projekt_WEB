Enchanted

A classic Disney fairytale is turned upside down when the beautiful princess Giselle is banished by an evil queen from her magical, musical animated land and finds herself in the gritty reality of modern-day Manhattan. Shocked by this strange new environment, Giselle soon falls in love with a charmingly imperfect divorce lawyer who comes to her aid.

Parents' Guide

Violence: Mild fairytale peril; a climax involving a dragon and some slapstick falls.

Language: Very clean; no profanity.

Sex: A few romantic kisses; no nudity or suggestive content.

Substances: No drug use or alcohol consumption by main characters.

Intensity: The transformation of the Queen into a dragon might be slightly scary for very young children.

Themes: True love, optimism, and adapting to change.

Messages: Real life can be just as magical as a fairytale if you have the right perspective.

Verdict: Recommended for all ages (G-rated equivalent); perfect family viewing.
