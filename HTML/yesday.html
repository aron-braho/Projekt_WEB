    Glass Onion

    Five long-time friends are invited to the Greek island home of billionaire Miles Bron. All five know Bron from way back and owe their current wealth, fame, and careers to him. The main event is a murder-weekend game with Bron to be the victim. In reality, they all have reasons to kill him. Also invited is Benoit Blanc, the world's greatest detective.

    Parents' Guide

    Violence & Gore

    There are several intense scenes involving action and conflict. Characters engage in fights, and some blood is shown, but there are no graphic injuries.

    Profanity

    The film contains mild language, including words like "damn" and "*****," used infrequently throughout the dialogue.

    Sex & Nudity

    There is a brief scene of kissing between two characters. No nudity is present, and the context is romantic.

    Alcohol, Drugs & Smoking

    Characters are depicted drinking alcohol in a celebratory context, but there is no excessive drinking or drug use shown.

    Frightening & Intense Scenes

    Some scenes may be frightening for younger viewers, particularly those involving dark settings and menacing creatures.

    Mature Themes

    The film explores themes of loss, friendship, and bravery, which may be complex for younger audiences to fully understand.

    Positive Messages

    The film promotes teamwork, courage, and the importance of standing up for what is right, making it a good choice for family viewing.

    Role Models

    The main character serves as a strong role model, demonstrating resilience and compassion throughout the story.

    Recommendations

    Recommended for ages 12 and up, with parental guidance suggested for younger viewers due to some intense scenes and themes.
