Mrs. Doubtfire

After a bitter divorce, Daniel Hillard, a talented but eccentric actor, loses custody of his children. Desperate to see them, he creates an elaborate alter ego: Euphegenia Doubtfire, a stern yet loving British nanny. As he integrates himself back into his family's life under a wig and latex mask, he learns more about being a father than he ever did as himself.

Parents' Guide

Violence: Comedic slapstick; a kitchen fire and some minor physical comedy mishaps.

Language: Occasional mild profanity and suggestive puns.

Sex: Brief suggestive humor regarding the disguise; no nudity.

Substances: Scenes involving social drinking in restaurants.

Intensity: Emotional scenes regarding divorce and family separation.

Themes: Family, identity, and the lengths a parent will go for their children.

Messages: Family looks different for everyone, but love remains the constant.

Verdict: Recommended for ages 10+ due to some mature comedic themes.
