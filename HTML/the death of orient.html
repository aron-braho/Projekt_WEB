Murder on the Orient Express

What starts out as a lavish train ride through Europe quickly unfolds into one of the most stylish, suspenseful, and thrilling mysteries ever told. Based on the novel by Agatha Christie, the story follows thirteen strangers stranded on a train, where everyone's a suspect. World-renowned detective Hercule Poirot must race against time to solve the puzzle before the murderer strikes again.

Parents' Guide

Violence: A murder occurs involving multiple stab wounds; a character is shot in the shoulder; brief depiction of a kidnapping backstory.

Language: Very mild profanity (e.g., "hell," "damn").

Sex: References to an extramarital affair; some mild suggestive comments; no nudity.

Substances: Social drinking of wine and spirits; use of a sedative to drug a character.

Intensity: Claustrophobic atmosphere and high-stakes emotional tension throughout the investigation.

Themes: Justice, revenge, and the moral complexity of the law.

Messages: Explores the difference between what is legal and what is right.

Verdict: Recommended for ages 12+ due to mature themes and the central crime.

Reviews

"A visually stunning adaptation of Christie's classic. Kenneth Branagh's mustache is a character of its own." - 4 Stars
