The Children's Train

Set in post-WWII Italy, a young boy from the impoverished streets of Naples is sent on a train to the north as part of a collective initiative to save children from poverty. Between a divided country and a split heart, he must navigate the meaning of family and the difficult choices of growing up.

Parents' Guide

Violence: Very minimal; some depictions of post-war poverty and emotional distress.

Language: Clean; mostly dramatic and emotional dialogue.

Sex: None.

Substances: Period-appropriate depictions of adults smoking.

Intensity: High emotional impact due to the themes of separation between children and their parents.

Themes: Sacrifice, social solidarity, poverty, and the search for identity.

Verdict: Rated PG-13 for emotional themes; a moving historical piece for mature children and adults.
